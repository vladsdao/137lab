"Shadow work — it's not a blowjob"

Misha · musician, producer · 4 months in Sintra

"This story is the start of a big book that's going to be a bestseller — that's what it's like to be inside the field of Vlad, Liza, and Arina."

The music I write and the creative ideas I can pass through me — vast, ambitious, fucking insane, on the level of Lennon's Imagine — that's what it's like to be inside the field of Vlad, Liza, and Arina.

In sessions with artists recording vocals, I hold their field, feel them, and help their voice open up in a way they have never heard before — to the point where they start crying when they sing — that's what it's like to be inside the field of Vlad, Liza, and Arina.

The music I make is so saturated with deep emotional colour that it grabs whoever's listening instantly, and the songs I sell move in a couple of days — that's what it's like to be inside the field of Vlad, Liza, and Arina.

I can finally feel — that's what it's like to be inside the field of Vlad, Liza, and Arina.

My story — beginning with gratitude

My story about moving to Portugal, Liza, Vlad, Arina, ancient Greek gods, consciousness from another dimension, alien civilisations, geopolitical questions resolved on the subtle plane — I want to begin with words of gratitude.

Gratitude on the level of awareness — release from a programme I had been living through many lives. Many. Gratitude on the level of feeling — finally free, on the physical and subtle planes, with the ability to do whatever I want on planet Earth. Gratitude toward these people — for acceptance, understanding, compassion.

It was the funniest, most playful, most life-filled, love-filled transformation — full of laughter and tears, screaming and quiet whispering. Thank you for changing my life.

Morning, the haze of "honesty"

I open my eyes in a room flooded with daylight from the Portuguese sun. The clock says 2pm, and there's a haze of "honesty" in the air. Vlad stands in the doorway, leaning on the handle, saying something to me. At first the words sound unclear, but as I rub my eyes I catch the anxious tone and the words about the absence of energy exchange with Liza.

The previous two nights begin to load in my head — the sessions with Vlad and Liza where, with all cards on the table and all dots connected, my shadow was being shown to me. Until the last moment I had refused to admit my real intentions to myself; finally I dared to see what my shadow actually was, and what I had been doing, unconsciously and sometimes consciously, for these whole 4 months — and how hard I had been suppressing the awareness of it. Insights open up; recent events flicker through my mind like film reel — only this time honestly, without the filter.

But let's start from the beginning. I'll share with you my story of transformation.

True transformation — it's quiet

"Transformation — it's a worn-out word, but a deep one. People started throwing it around left and right, beating their chests about how much they've transformed."

True transformation, in my experience, is quiet. To see and accept your shadow — that's the most important transformation in anyone's life. And how strongly we fear seeing it and admitting it.

For example: to see and admit that in your seemingly innocuous act of starting to chat with the friend who, sitting across from you on a chair with a laptop, is bringing his idea to life — there is a desire and intention to specifically distract him, to suppress his inspiration and spark, the same way yours was suppressed. And it's a loop.

Or to realise that your daily small decisions affect the person next to you — someone you don't actually love but feel safe with. And then choosing not to see it, not to look, not even glance in that direction, convincing yourself that the headache is from the weather, that the absence of sex is just a phase, that there's love and we'll manage. The list is endless.

And then, when you've seen all of it — to learn not to sink into guilt, but with full responsibility and awareness of your actions, with compassion, to accept and feel your real expression — the manifestation of your dark side in full "glory".

Late autumn — Lisbon, Sintra, Baileys

Late autumn, leaving the airport in Lisbon after a stunning 4-day party in Cyprus, I drag on a hand-rolled cigarette and book myself a taxi an hour from the airport. On the map — a dot, tucked into a green landscape near the ocean.

An hour later I see Liza sitting on the terrace, and Vlad coming out to me with a bottle of Baileys, hugging me tight like an old friend. And right here is where my transformation began.

By the evening the three of us are sitting in a circle near the fireplace, and a ceremony is happening. Back then I had no idea what that even was, but I was making a show of taking active part. My return ticket was in 5 days, and the whole thing looked, to me, like a 5-day visit to friends in Portugal. As you already know, I never went back.

The bandwidth of consciousness

Through a month of regular daily practices, expansion of consciousness, and several trips, my extrasensory abilities start opening up. Not the kind they show on TV. Real ones — real enough that I kept testing them, like a teenage schoolboy who suddenly learned to read minds and is now eavesdropping on what his classmates are thinking.

"What it really is — is bandwidth, the volume that consciousness can carry. The more of your shadow you see, the deeper you go in practice — the wider the bandwidth."

The funeral of old Misha

"This morning we're going to bury you," Vlad says. "Get ready, write down everything you're saying goodbye to. Realise that we are burying the old you, but remember — old Misha will keep trying to come back, and it's very important to watch for that. Watch the intention, watch what you think and how you think about it."

Slightly stunned by what I just heard, I nod and agree. I had heard about practices like this somewhere, but I never could have imagined it would happen to me. Even now I can say — it was the best send-off of an old self. Beautiful. No wasted words. I asked them to play The Doors' "The End". I wrote on a piece of paper everyone and everything I was saying goodbye to, energetically and otherwise, gave the note to Vlad, and went to prepare.

5am, sunrise. Vlad leads me, blindfolded, to the grave on the property. It's a pit dug about a metre deep, with a hinged wooden door fitted as a lid. Ceremony, The Doors, flowers. I'm lying in the cold earth; I hear Liza speaking farewell words to me in a tender tone; Vlad reads out my note; the door closes, the singing bowl rings.

When the lid closes over you lying there, and then you hear earth falling onto it — no matter how hard the brain resists, the body lives the funeral in real time, so to speak. About 15-20 minutes later, hearing mantras, words, instructions from Vlad "above", I start to fade out. Like losing consciousness, only very slowly. 10 seconds, 15, 20, breath. I open my eyes. I hear no sound. Silence. The state — like I had slept for 3 days.

I try to get up. I start pushing the lid of the grave. "Vlad, help me," I say. "You have to come out yourself, come on." I lie back down. With my foot I push the lid out and climb out. In dirty clothes and my green cap. My first decision — burn the cap, as the image of the old self. Done.

"I look at the world with different eyes. When I see events or actions, even the smallest details take on a different meaning. Phew, this is transformation, holy shit," I thought — not realising this was only the beginning of the path.

Career. Music. Subtlety.

Around this time, the transformation begins to unfold in my reality, in my life, in my career. I let go of 90% of my artists. I start to notice which work I had been making from inspiration, and where I'd been on autopilot. The autopilot was almost everywhere.

I begin to feel differently — more subtly. To see the connections between emotions, chords, feelings, and resonances. How a single A minor chord, for instance, can hold an unbelievable amount of different charge — different emotional impulses. And to know clearly what to do in a song to make it sound real. In sessions with artists, I begin to feel the artist's state during recording, and through what I say to them, direct that state up or down.

The world of Gods

For roughly the first two months I plunge into a completely different world. The world of Gods. A God is the one who creates their own reality. Channelings, practices, sessions, figures, Olympus, ancient civilisations, alien beings, technogenic planets, connections, contracts.

Through the practices, the sessions, and life inside the field with these people — who, in fact, are spiritual teachers — my constantly expanding bandwidth of consciousness begins to let through more and more knowledge, memories from past lives, images and consciousnesses. I hear about the Tao, Hermeticism, Thoth, Ancient Egypt, the Quantum Field, Quantum DNA customisation, and learn what really happened at Pushkin's duel.

In deep practices, the three of us live through civilisational suffering, and crack open the backend of creation on the subtle plane. My perception of creativity in general grows exponentially. Liza and Vlad, in the sessions, reflash my old base settings and blocks, tear up contracts of energy drain and service to egregores. I called this "spiritual Navy SEALs". Everything happens with utmost precision, coordination — and at the same time with great love and support.

A week feels like a month. A month — like a year. I had never felt this kind of speed, never seen it.

"Never seen it in this life?" Liza asks me. Pillows, fireplace, candles, a jug of water with lemon. "Yes, in this one," I answer. And immediately ask myself: in past ones — did I see it? Yes, it resonates. "Just in this life I haven't seen it, in past ones I have," I tell Liza, opening my eyes. I look at her; she silently smiles and nods.

Of every ceremony, every channeling, every session — you could write a book, with characters, scenery, and emotional density.

Game B

One of the most important steps was the transition to Game B — recognising my patriarchal nature and disidentifying from it. The transition out of Game A — where competition, violence, subjugation, exploitation, and the principle "I won only when you lost" run the show — into Game B, where the core principle is synergy, resonance, and coherence between people. Where everyone moves from a place of inspiration, insight, and resonance toward another person, the body, nature. They build together, create together, enjoy life.

Admitting to myself that all my life, as a man, I had operated on the strategy of "bros before hoes", and seeing how every one of my actions had been done to uphold that structure — was painful. And nothing close to easy.

The wildest school

"The wildest fucking school of transformation — though not a school in the usual sense. It's when you're being thick, you fuck up, you make mistakes — and they just hold you, without judgement, without verdicts. That's when real transformation happens…"
